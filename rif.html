Beginner FAQ

Five questions almost every new player asks. Answers follow the official Core Rules and the March 2026 tournament update.

What exactly is Riftbound, and how does it differ from Hearthstone or Magic?

Riftbound is a physical TCG developed by Riot Games and published with UVS Games. It supports 2–4 players, and matches revolve around sending your champion, units, and spells to capture battlefields. The first player to reach 8 Conquest points wins. Unlike MTG's land system, Riftbound uses a rune pool that refills on a fixed schedule, and unlike Hearthstone it supports multi-player free-for-all and team formats right out of the box.

How much does it cost to get started?

The lowest entry point is a single Champion Deck (about $25–30). Each one is fully preconstructed around a single champion and ready to play straight out of the box. Two friends with one deck each can start playing immediately. If you want to explore deckbuilding, add a Proving Grounds 2-player starter ($40) or a Booster Box ($120+).

Which sets exist, and where should I start?

As of spring 2026 there are three major releases: Origins (OGN, the launch set), Proving Grounds / Spiritforged (SFD), and Unleashed (UNL, the newest expansion featuring Vi and Vex among others). New players can simply grab the most recent Champion Deck from Unleashed — all sets are cross-compatible.

Can I play online without a local opponent?

Yes. Community tools like Riftscribe, RiftCore, and the Riftbot Discord bot cover card databases, deckbuilding, and match tracking. Runes & Rift runs free weekly online tournaments every Saturday. There's no official client yet, but between Tabletop Simulator and community platforms the online scene is already mature.

Seven cards were banned in March 2026 — does that affect beginners?

No. The ban list targets oppressive cards in the competitive metagame, and every card inside an official Champion Deck remains fully legal. For your first few months, metagame shifts don't matter. If you want to track the current banned list, check riftbound.gg's announcements page.
